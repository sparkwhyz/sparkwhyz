School Partnerships

Helping students become fire-safety leaders.

SparkWhyz works with schools to bring age-appropriate fire-prevention education, classroom books, student leadership, and community action to young people.

School Partnership Photo Coming Soon
Why Schools Matter

Prevention education works best before a dangerous decision.

Young people may not fully understand how quickly fireworks can cause injuries, property damage, or wildfires.

Schools provide an important opportunity to reach students early, give them clear information, and help them understand the consequences of one impulsive choice.

SparkWhyz uses storytelling, student leadership, classroom materials, and community partnerships to make fire-safety education memorable and relevant.

What We Bring to Schools

Education students can understand and remember.

SparkWhyz school programs are designed to be practical, age appropriate, and supported by students, educators, families, and fire-safety professionals.

Classroom Education

Age-appropriate lessons help students understand fireworks risks, wildfire prevention, and responsible decision-making.

Children’s Book

The SparkWhyz children’s book helps students learn through storytelling, discussion, illustrations, and real community experiences.

Student Leadership

Students can lead pledge drives, presentations, outreach projects, events, social media, and school chapters.

Frequently Asked Questions

School partnership questions.

What grade levels can participate?

SparkWhyz materials can be adapted for elementary, middle school, and high school students. Each group should receive content appropriate for its maturity and learning level.

Can a teacher request a classroom visit?

Yes. Teachers and administrators can contact SparkWhyz to discuss book readings, presentations, pledge campaigns, or classroom activities.

Can students outside Laguna Beach participate?

Yes. SparkWhyz began in Laguna Beach, but students from other schools and communities can express interest in starting a chapter or using SparkWhyz educational materials.

How are classroom books funded?

Classroom books may be supported through sponsors, grants, donations, school partners, and community fundraising.

Does a school need to start a chapter?

No. Schools can request a presentation, book reading, classroom program, pledge campaign, or one-time event without creating a permanent chapter.
