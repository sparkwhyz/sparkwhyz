2nd Fireworks Turn-In Event

SparkWhyz continued its prevention mission by supporting another community fireworks turn-in event.

The event gave residents a safer and more responsible alternative to keeping or using illegal fireworks. It also created an opportunity for students and families to learn about wildfire risk and community safety.

Through youth outreach, public education, and collaboration with local fire-safety leaders, the event reinforced a simple message: prevention begins before the spark.

Community Coverage

Laguna Beach comes together for prevention.

SparkWhyz’s citywide fireworks turn-in effort brought together students, families, firefighters, city leaders, and community supporters.

The event showed that prevention can be positive, practical, and community-driven.

Rather than waiting for fireworks to cause a fire, injury, or emergency, residents were encouraged to make a safer choice before something happened.

The event also helped SparkWhyz share its youth-led message with more families across Laguna Beach.

Our Founding Story

One question started everything.

SparkWhyz began after a wildfire was started in Laguna Beach by a middle-school-aged boy using illegal fireworks.

Instead of asking only who should be blamed, our family asked a different question: what if someone had reached that student before he made the decision?

That question became the foundation of SparkWhyz.

The mission is to reach young people earlier, give them safer choices, and help them understand that one impulsive action can affect an entire community.
